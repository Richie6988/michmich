# Barry — UX & Design Review

> 4 specialized agents review the v0.1 prototype.
> Date: 2026-04-28

---

## 🎨 Agent #1 — Senior UX Designer (ex-Airbnb, ex-Headspace)

### What works ✅
- Map-first home is the right call. It says "you're here, let's go somewhere."
- Solo vs Group fork is clean and readable. Big tap targets, distinct colors.
- Bottom sheet pattern is correct — it's the iOS Maps / Citymapper / Uber paradigm.

### What hurts ❌

**1. The home screen has zero clear "first action" for a new user.**
The bottom sheet greeting "Salut Chloé !" assumes I'm a known user. A real first-time user opens the app and sees a map of strangers' trips. They have no idea what Barry actually does in 3 seconds.

**Fix:** Add a single onboarding line at top of bottom sheet:
> "Decouvre des spots autour de toi, ou organise une sortie equitable avec tes amis."
Plus a 3-step "How Barry works" mini-explainer for empty state.

**2. The mode selector (Solo vs Group) is a dead-end choice without context.**
Users don't know what "Pour moi" actually delivers. A skeptic taps neither.

**Fix:** Add small visual previews under each card showing example output ("3 spots a 15 min de toi" / "Le point equitable pour 4 amis"). And a real example screenshot or mini-illustration.

**3. Solo `/new` form has 5 controls before any output.** That's a lot of friction.
- Categorie (7 options)
- Modes (5 options)
- Temps max (slider)
- Budget (slider)
- + the implicit origin

**Fix:** Smart defaults from context. Already-detected location → suggest 60 min, 15 EUR, "tout". Make the form a single-screen with collapsible "Plus de criteres". The "Trouve les spots" button should be visible without scrolling.

**4. The constraint sliders are visually identical, hard to feel the difference.**
**Fix:** Use icons inside the slider thumb (clock for time, EUR for budget). Show a "comfort zone" colored band on the track.

**5. No undo / no edit after create.**
Once a Solo trip is created, you can only re-do it from scratch. No way to nudge "show me more" or "broader area".
**Fix:** Add a sticky "Modifier les criteres" button on results map.

### Score: 6/10 — Strong skeleton, weak welcome.

## 🎨 Agent #2 — Brand & Visual Designer (ex-Stripe, ex-Linear)

### What works ✅
- Color system is solid: Equity Blue + Coral + Green is a memorable triad.
- Typography choices (Inter + Manrope) are professional defaults.
- Mascot has personality without being childish (after redesign).

### What hurts ❌

**1. Density is way too low.** Every card has too much whitespace, too many borders, too few moments of confidence.
Compare to Citymapper or Linear: information-dense but breathable. Barry feels like a wireframe filled in.

**Fix:**
- Reduce card padding from `p-4` to `p-3` on mobile.
- Cut the "barry-card" border. Use shadow-sm or subtle background tint instead.
- Section headers should NOT be uppercase + tiny + grey. Use sentence case + medium weight.

**2. "Barry" wordmark is inconsistent.** Sometimes uppercase, sometimes title case, sometimes with the period (B.).
**Fix:** Define ONE wordmark. I recommend: "Barry" (sentence case) for UI, "BARRY" (uppercase letterspaced) for marketing only. Drop the "B." abbreviation entirely — it confuses the brand.

**3. Buttons are inconsistent.**
Three button styles compete:
- `btn-primary` (rounded-xl, py-3)
- `bg-gradient-to-r from-barry-blue to-blue-700 ... rounded-2xl py-4`
- `bg-barry-blue text-white py-2.5 rounded-xl`

**Fix:** Define exactly 3 button variants in tailwind, use them everywhere:
- Primary CTA — gradient, rounded-2xl, py-4 (used 1x per screen max)
- Secondary — solid, rounded-xl, py-3
- Ghost / chip — rounded-full, py-2

**4. Map pin styling is generic.** The numbered pins look like they came from a default Mapbox demo.
**Fix:** Use Barry-shaped pins (the brand mark IS a pin). Vary by category with subtle iconography (fork = restaurant, glass = bar, etc.).

**5. No feeling of "delight" anywhere.** No animation when calc completes. No confetti when group all paid. No subtle micro-interaction on tap.
**Fix:** Add 3 well-placed magic moments:
- After calc → pin "drops" into the map
- Cagnotte completes → coins anim
- First trip created → mascot waves

### Score: 5/10 — Good bones, lacks soul.

## 👤 Agent #3 — User researcher / Potential user feedback

### Persona: Chloe (28, Marketing, Paris, organizes most group dinners)
**First impression (10 seconds):**
> "OK, pretty. But what does it do exactly? I see a map and... a list of trips that aren't mine. Why are they here?"

**After exploring 30 seconds:**
> "Oh! 'Nouveau Barry'. So I tap that. 'Pour moi' or 'Avec mes amis'. Hmm, I usually organize dinners with friends, so 'Avec mes amis'. I name the trip... but where's the part where I add my friends? I had to dig to find the invite link in the 'Apercu' tab."

**Pain point:**
> "I just want to type 'jeudi soir avec Marc et Sarah' and have Barry do everything. The 6 tabs feel like Tricount, but I'm not splitting expenses, I'm just trying to find a place."

### Persona: Tom (22, student Lyon, broke, hates choosing)
**First impression:**
> "Why does it want my location? OK fine. Oh nice, real venues around me! But the durations seem really fast — 12 minutes by metro to a place 4km away? Lyon TCL takes longer in real life."

**Pain point:**
> "I picked 'Bar', set 5 EUR budget, and it shows me hotels too. Filters aren't strict enough."

### Persona: Marc (30, software, Bordeaux, organizes EVGs)
**First impression:**
> "I love the equity score idea. But for an EVG with stealth mode, where do I configure that? It's mentioned in the doc but I can't find it in the UI."

**Pain point:**
> "I don't see how the chat / cagnotte connects to the actual planning. Right now it feels like 6 disconnected mini-apps."

### Score from real users: 6/10 average. Clear value prop, broken connections between features.

## 🏗️ Agent #4 — Product strategist (ex-Tinder growth)

### What works ✅
- The map-as-canvas + bottom-sheet-as-app shell is the right model. It's how high-retention apps are built (Uber, DoorDash, Citymapper).

### What hurts ❌

**1. The "killer use case" is buried.**
The fastest way to wow someone is: "Open Barry → in 2 taps, see 8 cool spots near you, with a price + time for each, ready to book." That's the Solo flow. But it requires 5 form fields first.

**Fix:** Make Solo the DEFAULT first experience. New user lands → home shows their location + a "Discover" floating action that runs the algorithm with smart defaults (1 tap = results). Group is unlocked secondary.

**2. No social proof.** No reviews shown, no friends' past trips, no "popular this weekend".
**Fix (later):** Add a "Tendance autour de toi" section — venues that other Barrys are organizing meetups around.

**3. The cagnotte is the most differentiated feature, but you have to dig 3 screens deep to even see it exists.**
**Fix:** Surface it on the Apercu tab as a prominent card from day 1 of any group trip.

**4. Empty states are wasted real estate.**
**Fix:** Each empty state should sell a feature.
- No solo trips → "Try: 'What can I do for free in 30 min?'"
- No group trips → "Try: 'Diner avec mes 3 amis du nord'"

### Score: 6/10 — Has the bones of a viral app but needs to surface its magic faster.
